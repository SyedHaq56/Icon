BIKE ACCIDENTS IN THE BRONX HAVE SPIKED UP

Bikers and pedestrians injured in traffic collisions stood alongside family members of others who died in similar accidents, demanding the city create more protected bike lanes in The Bronx on Sept. 16. Transportation Alternatives joined Families For Safe Streets in organizing the vigil. "We stand with our same heavy eyes and we have the same moments of silence, and we hear the same words," said Danny Harris, 41, Executive Director of Transportation Alternatives, addressing the victims. "We have to go to vigil after vigil to simply get our mayor to take the action that he had committed to, by advancing Vision Zero."

Vision Zero is a citywide initiative that aims to prevent traffic deaths through improved engineering, enforcement and education.
There have been 521 bicycle injuries in the borough this year, an increase of 377 from this time last year, according to that source. Between April and August, 372 injuries to bikers in traffic accidents in the Bronx were reported.
Through August, there have been 37 traffic fatalities in the Bronx, compared with 19 fatalities last year, according to NYPD's TrafficStat.
